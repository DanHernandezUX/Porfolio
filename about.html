About Me

First and foremost, it's nice to meet you.

When I'm not working on design, I can be often be found in four places: In the gym at the crack of dawn, on an airplane seat, in the kitchen recreating recipes from my childhood, or on the couch with a controller. I think video games are finally getting their spotlight as a great story telling medium, and I'm interested in exploring VR as a UX avenue.

How I Got Started

I dabbled in marketing and sales in the beginning of my career. I started out designing print items such as brochures and sell sheets for sales teams. A line that I have always kept close to me has been "know your audience."

Relatively quickly, my design skills were transferred to digital and I began designing websites for businesses focused on transitioning to mobile responsive formats. I picked up some coding skills, and more importantly, I discovered the UX design process along the way; It's helped me become a better communicator and more inspired creative while keeping function at the heart of it all.

UX is my perfect blend of techinical creativity and "knowing your audience."

What Makes Me Tick

I'm curious. I like to ask questions. I think the best designers don't make a single change until they've taken the time to fully understand why something was made the way it is. We can always stand to take more time listening.
