Retirement Services Microsites


Overview

The purpose of this project was to create a digital journey to guide users through an experience that would lead them to a seldom used free financial fitness module with resources.

Goal

Improve the number of participants who registered and interacted with the module.

Role

UX/UI Design and implementation for the web elements.

Responsibility

Wireframe and design web templates, code template.

Timeline

The project timeline was 3 weeks.

The Project:

The marketing team from the retirement services business engaged our team to assist in a campaign to encourage users to interact with a free module within their online retirement service. The purpose of the module is to help assess current financial fitness and provide free resources tailored to the age and income bracket of the user.

The marketing team wanted to focus on targeting 3 separate age groups based on loosely defined personas from 18-34, 35-50 and 50+. Plan participants within each age group would receive an email linking them to a microsite with articles catered to their age group. For example, the 50+ crowd had articles geared toward understanding social security, while the 18-34 group would have articles focused on budgeting and understanding debt repayments.

Solving For Engagement

The Intended Solution

The issue identified was that engagement with the module was low. The module is hosted behind the secured log-in environment and is difficult to find. From gathered data, we also were able to note that users do not typically log in to their accounts more than a handful of times per year. The approach identified was to create a micro-journey that would entice different user groups with free teaser content relevant to their respective age group and link them directly to the module.

Every Project is Not Perfect

A Flaw in the Plan

In order for this solution to operate correctly the user would need to be redirected to the module from the log in screen once they engaged in the call to action. Unfortunately, due to technical limitations we were unaware of at the time, IT was unable to create a redirect to the module from the log in because of a third party authentication service that the retirement service uses. When the user clicked the CTA - they would be just brought to the log in page and then be brought to their retirement plan homescreen, and not the module.

The Short Term Solution

Unfortunately given the tight timeline and constraints, we decided that a version 1 solution of this instance could be to add a small banner that would contain images and text similar to what their experience had been from the email and webpages they had visited to help guide the user.

Take Aways and Next Steps

Get To the Table Earlier

Marketing brought the digital experience team in on this project with only a few weeks left before launching. Getting to the table earlier could have provided us more opportunity to pivot to a more appropriate solution for the final outcome and evaluate the solution as a whole. Since this lives on a Salesforce Marketing Cloud microsite, things like SEO and continuity from the main website are out the window.

Research is Essential for Improvement

Since this was the first time putting out these types of articles, we would like to understand the efficacy of the content for each age group. Was this the most desired content for their age group, or were there other topics that are more appealing? A follow up survey is something we would like to include for the next iteration to gauge interest levels.

Gather and Analyze the Data

Understanding what effect this had on the number of interactions with the module and tracking are something our group would like to better understand for the next iteration. We are looking forward to seeing the data and understanding what variables we can alter to improve such as time of year to launch, or number of stories offered.
